Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with rich sauces and fillings like seasoned ground meat, tomato sauce, béchamel (white sauce), and cheese—usually mozzarella and parmesan. It's baked in the oven until the layers meld into a flavorful, hearty casserole with a bubbling, golden top.

Traditionally served as a main course, lasagna is known for its comforting taste and satisfying texture. Variations exist across regions and diets—such as vegetarian versions with spinach, mushrooms, or zucchini. It's a popular dish for gatherings, as it can be prepared in advance and served in generous portions.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Dried herbs

Steps

  1. Cook meat sauce: Sauté onion, garlic, and ground meat. Add tomato sauce, herbs, salt, and pepper. Simmer until thick.
  2. Make béchamel sauce: Melt butter, stir in flour, then slowly add milk. Cook until smooth and thick.
  3. Boil noodles(skip if oven-ready): Cook lasagna sheets until al dente and drain.
  4. Assemble layers: In a baking dish, layer meat sauce, noodles, béchamel, and cheese. Repeat 2–3 times.
  5. Bake: Cover with foil and bake at 180°C (350°F) for 30 mins. Uncover and bake 15–20 mins more until golden.
  6. Rest and serve: Let it sit 10-15 mins before slicing.
