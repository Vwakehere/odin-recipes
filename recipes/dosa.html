Dosa

Description

Dosa is a thin, crispy South Indian crepe made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. It's known for its light texture, golden color, and slightly tangy flavor. Traditionally served hot with coconut chutney and sambar, dosa is a staple breakfast but can be enjoyed any time of day.

Its versatility allows for variations like Masala Dosa (filled with spiced potato) or Rava Dosa (made from semolina). Despite its delicate appearance, dosa is surprisingly filling and is loved for its crispy bite and wholesome ingredients.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Urad dal
  • Fenugreek seeds
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Oil/Ghee(for cooking)

Steps

  1. Soak and grind: Soak rice, dal, and fenugreek separately for 4–6 hours. Grind to a smooth batter.
  2. Ferment: Mix, cover, and ferment overnight (8–12 hrs) until doubled and bubbly.
  3. Prepare batter: Add salt and water to adjust consistency (like pancake batter).
  4. Cook dosa: Heat a flat pan, spread a ladle of batter thin, drizzle oil, and cook until crisp. No need to flip.
  5. Serve: With chutney and sambar.
